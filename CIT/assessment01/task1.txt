a)	What are the main characteristics of scripting languages?

    Scripting languages are normally simplified (high level) compared to programming languages which are more low level.
    They are typically interpreted rather than compiled, and are likely to contain a list of commands that can be used for achieving certain goals
    in an operating system or other environment (for example, setting configurations on hardware or software).
